# LBS Intelligence System
## Product Requirements Document

**Version 1.0 | April 2026 | Internal Use Only**

---

## What It Is

The LBS Intelligence System is an internal analytics platform that automatically pulls data from our store, paid media, and search platforms, analyzes it weekly, and delivers a single briefing document: what changed, what it means, and what to do about it.

It is not a dashboard. It is a weekly analyst report generated by software — backed by real data, written in plain English, and ending in specific action items.

---

## The Problem

Our performance data lives across six platforms: BigCommerce, Google Analytics, Google Ads, Google Search Console, Meta Ads, and Klaviyo. Getting a complete picture of the business requires manually checking all of them, cross-referencing numbers that don't naturally talk to each other, and drawing conclusions without a systematic framework.

The result is that non-obvious problems go unnoticed for weeks:

- A product getting strong traffic but failing at checkout due to a shipping cost issue
- An entire brand's search performance dropping after a product data update
- A paid campaign quietly bleeding spend while conversions decline
- High-intent buyers arriving from AI tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity with no visibility into which products or pages are winning

The Intelligence System surfaces these automatically, every week, before they compound.

---

## How It Works

1. **Ingest** — Scheduled jobs pull the past 7 days of data from each connected platform into a centralized database
2. **Analyze** — Purpose-built modules compute metrics, detect changes, and flag anomalies using deterministic rules
3. **Report** — An AI layer converts the structured findings into a plain-English briefing with ranked action items

The AI writes the narrative. The analytics are rules-based, auditable, and traceable back to source data. No black-box conclusions.

---

## Three-Phase Delivery

### Phase 1 — Foundation
**Data sources:** Google Analytics 4, Google Search Console, Google Ads, BigCommerce

**Weekly report answers:**
- Which products have strong traffic but poor checkout conversion? *(Specific SKU list, ranked)*
- Which brands are underperforming across their catalog this week?
- Where is the checkout funnel leaking and by how much?
- What changed in paid search performance and what is the likely cause?
- Are AI tools sending us meaningful traffic and revenue?
- What are the top 5 action items this week?

---

### Phase 2 — Growth
**Data sources added:** Meta Ads, Klaviyo, vendor fulfillment tracking

**New capabilities:**
- Email flow and campaign performance — which sends drive revenue vs. just clicks
- Meta Ads performance contextualized against on-site outcomes
- Vendor reliability scoring — flag vendors whose fulfillment lag is increasing before it generates negative reviews or chargebacks
- Keyword gap detection — surface high-volume search queries we appear for but have no strong product targeting, with white label product brief opportunities

---

### Phase 3 — AI Visibility
**Data sources added:** AI citation monitoring API (Exa/Tavily)

**New capabilities:**
- Track whether LBS pages and products are cited in AI assistant answers (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini)
- Competitive presence in AI responses for key product categories
- AI visibility scorecard alongside traditional search metrics

## What the Weekly Report Looks Like

The following are representative examples of the findings the system will surface.

---

**PRODUCT OPPORTUNITY — Action Required**

> *SKU XY-XLJC4F50WSCSBLVS-E received 167 product page views this week with a 76% add-to-cart rate — indicating strong buying intent. However, only 26 of 127 cart additions converted to a completed purchase (20% checkout conversion). This pattern is consistent with a shipping cost or pricing friction issue at checkout. Recommended action: review shipping cost display and final checkout price for this SKU. Compare against nearest competitor pricing. Priority: this week.*

Outcome: Team reviews the listing, finds shipping cost is displaying higher than expected for certain zip codes, adjusts the shipping rule. Conversion recovers the following week.

---

**BRAND ALERT — Investigate**

> *Satco products declined 31% in add-to-cart rate this week across 44 active SKUs (from 18% to 12% blended). The drop is consistent across the brand rather than isolated to specific products, suggesting a catalog-level change rather than an individual listing issue. Possible causes: recent price adjustment, product feed update affecting titles or descriptions, or a competitor pricing shift. Recommended action: audit Satco product data for recent changes and compare pricing on top 10 SKUs. Priority: this week.*

Outcome: Team discovers a recent bulk product description update changed spec formatting, which was degrading listing quality. Update is reverted.

---

**PAID SEARCH — Monitor**

> *Google Ads lost 22% of eligible impressions this week. Of that loss: 18 points were due to ad rank (quality score or bid issue) and 4 points due to budget. The rank-based loss increased 8 points week-over-week. Recommended action: review Quality Scores on highest-spend campaigns. Budget loss is minor and does not require action this week. Priority: monitor.*

---

**AI REFERRAL — Opportunity**

> *Perplexity.ai sent 43 sessions this week with a 4.2% purchase conversion rate and $1,840 in revenue — outperforming our organic search conversion rate of 2.8%. Top landing page: LED High Bay collection. This channel grew 40% week-over-week. No action required. Track for continued growth and consider which pages are best positioned for AI citation.*

## Technology

- **Backend:** Python
- **Database:** Supabase (PostgreSQL) — all data stored internally, not shared with third parties
- **Dashboard:** Streamlit — internal web UI for drilling into findings
- **AI layer:** Claude API (Anthropic) — report narrative generation only, not analytics
- **Hosting:** Internal / GitHub Actions scheduled jobs

No proprietary business data leaves our infrastructure except to the Claude API for report generation, which operates under Anthropic's enterprise data handling policy.
